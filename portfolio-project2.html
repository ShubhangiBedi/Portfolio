SaaS Sales Pipeline

Behind every lead, sales call, and opportunity lies a reason for success or failure. Guided by data and structured frameworks, I analyzed TechnoServe’s SaaS sales pipeline to uncover why qualified leads weren’t converting — and how to turn insights into higher win rates.

  1. Home
  2. Project Details

Frameworks Applied

  • 5W Framework: Defined who, what, when, where, and how conversions fail.
  • SPIN Selling: Identified situation, problem, implication, and need-payoff questions guiding analysis.
  • 5C Hypothesis Framework: Built an issue tree covering customer, company, competition, collaborators, and context.

Key Insights:

  • Technology: “Legacy Modernization” has the lowest win rate (12.15%).
  • Sales Medium: Online leads convert at only 6.46%, while Enterprise Sellers perform best.
  • Client Profile: Small clients (< $25K last year) had the highest conversion (83%).
  • Sales Cycle: Engagement beyond 93 days → 100% conversion.
  • Targeting: Zero-revenue clients were most targeted but least likely to convert.

Recommendations:

  • Prioritize clients with past revenue (especially $0–25K segment).
  • Encourage engagement beyond 90 days for stronger conversion chances.
  • Deprioritize “Online Leads” and shift focus to high-performing channels.
  • Avoid allocating resources to zero-revenue clients with low win rates.
By turning raw CRM data into structured insights, this project provides TechnoServe with a roadmap to optimize targeting, improve sales efficiency, and boost pipeline conversion rates
