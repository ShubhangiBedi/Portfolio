Ola Booking Insights

In the world of urban mobility, every ride tells a story, but behind every smooth journey lies data-driven decision making. As Bengaluru’s bustling streets set the stage, the challenge for ride-hailing services wasn’t just about moving people—it was about optimizing bookings, reducing cancellations, and matching supply with demand. That’s where the Ola dataset became the steering wheel, turning raw trip data into roadmaps for smarter operations and happier riders.

Objective:

The project set out to decode the patterns of ride-booking behavior by analyzing Ola ride data from Bengaluru over a one-month period. From identifying peak hours and high-demand zones to uncovering cancellation triggers and revenue drivers, the goal was to equip Ola with actionable insights to improve efficiency and customer satisfaction.

Problem Statement

In a competitive ride-hailing market, intuition alone cannot ensure growth. Ola needed to know: What times and areas experience the highest demand? Why do cancellations happen—and who drives them more? Which vehicle types maximize revenue and rider satisfaction? This project dives into those questions, transforming raw booking data into strategies for higher reliability and profitability.

Data & Methodology

The dataset covered 20,000+ Ola rides across Bengaluru, capturing details such as booking status, vehicle type, pickup and drop areas, distance, fare, and payment method. Using SQL, Excel, and Power BI, the analysis included:

  • Data cleaning (handling duplicates, missing values, and inconsistent entries)
  • Segmentation by booking status, vehicle type, and geography
  • Trend analysis by hour, day, and event periods (e.g., weekends)
  • Calculation of KPIs including booking success rate, cancellation causes, and average ride metrics

Summarized Insights:

  1. Booking Success Rate: Only ~62% of bookings were successful; cancellations remain a critical pain point.
  2. Customer Cancellations: ~25% of customer cancellations were influenced by drivers requesting riders to cancel.
  3. Driver Cancellations: >29% of driver cancellations were due to customer-related issues, pointing to the need for rider behavior monitoring.
  4. Peak Ride Hours: Demand spikes during 8–10 AM and 6–9 PM, highlighting commuter patterns.
  5. High-Demand Areas: Banashankari, Kammanahalli, KR Puram, Peenya, and Sarjapur Road recorded the maximum traffic.

Recommendations

  • Improve Success Rate: Optimize driver allocation and app interface to reduce booking failures.
  • Control Cancellations: Introduce stricter penalties for cancellation manipulation and incentivize compliance.
  • Enhance Rider Behavior: Use a transparent rating/education system to reduce customer-driven cancellations.
  • Peak-Time Strategy: Apply dynamic pricing and driver incentives during morning/evening commuter rush.
  • Geographic Expansion: Increase fleet density in high-demand areas to meet rising urban mobility needs.

In the final lap, this project demonstrates how the fusion of mobility data and analytics can steer Ola toward smarter operations and improved customer trust. For Bengaluru’s commuters, these insights don’t just map rides—they pave the way for a more reliable, efficient, and rider-friendly future.
