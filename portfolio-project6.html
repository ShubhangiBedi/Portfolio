Bank Telemarketing Campaign

Turning noise into signal, this project decodes a bank's telemarketing data to uncover the hidden patterns of customer engagement and drive targeted, data-backed growth.

  1. Home
  2. Bank Telemarketing Campaign

Objective:

The main objective of this project is to identify the ideal target customers and the key driving factors behind a successful customer conversion for future marketing campaigns conducted by the Bank of Corporate.

Problem Statement:

Bank of Corporate launched a telemarketing campaign for its 'Term deposits' product. The challenge is to analyze the campaign data to identify target customers from the bank's existing pool and to capture the variables that led to a successful conversion, i.e., a customer opening a term deposit account.

Data & Methodology:

The analysis is based on a dataset from the Bank of Corporate's telemarketing campaign. The methodology involves a comprehensive univariate and bivariate analysis of key variables. Univariate analysis examined single variables like Age, Job Categories, and Marital Status to understand their distribution and characteristics. Bivariate analysis explored the relationship between these variables and the campaign's success (Subscription outcome) to uncover influential factors. The analysis was conducted to uncover patterns and actionable insights for future campaigns.

Summarized Insights:

  • Customer Demographics: The age group of 35-60 years forms the largest customer base and also shows a higher conversion rate, while those over 60 convert poorly. Married customers dominate the customer base (60.21%), but their conversion rate is low at 5.91%.
  • Occupation & Education: Blue-collar employees constitute the largest group of customers, but they have the highest number of rejections (9,130). Management professionals had the highest number of successful subscriptions (1,272).While master's degree holders generate the most subscriptions (2,385), they also have the highest number of rejections (21,006).
  • Campaign Factors: The call duration is a critical success driver; conversions are concentrated around calls lasting 229+ seconds. Calls made via a cellular contact method significantly outperformed the telephone method, with a conversion rate of 9.34% versus 0.84%.
  • Timing: The second quarter (April, May, June) is the best for conversions, with May being the peak month with 13,956 total contacts. The first quarter and December showed very weak performance. Wednesdays had the highest rate of both "yes" and "no" responses, indicating high engagement.
  • Financial Characteristics: Customers with existing housing loans dominate the customer base, but their conversion is lower due to their existing financial burden. Targeting loan defaulters is a waste of resources, as they show a near-complete rejection rate (98.18%).

Recommendations:

  • Refine Targeting: Prioritize marketing efforts on the 35-60 age group, and customers with management or technician job roles, specifically those without loan defaults. De-prioritize targeting customers who are 60+, loan defaulters, and those with existing loans.
  • Improve Call Strategy: Train telemarketing agents to extend conversations beyond 200 seconds. Use tailored, relationship-building scripts, especially for married couples and blue-collar workers, to improve their conversion rates.
  • Optimize Timing: Schedule campaigns to run primarily in the second quarter, particularly from May to July. Focus calls on mid-week days, avoiding Fridays.
  • Channel Focus: Increase the use of cellular-based outreach and reduce reliance on telephones. Explore using digital nudges like SMS or email for follow-ups.

In conclusion, this project demonstrates how leveraging data analytics can transform a telemarketing campaign from a broad-based effort into a highly targeted and efficient strategy. By focusing on the identified key drivers, the Bank of Corporate can significantly improve its conversion rates, optimize resource allocation, and build stronger relationships with its most receptive customers.
